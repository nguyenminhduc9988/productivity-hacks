15 ChatGPT Prompts for Solopreneurs to Run a One-Person Business in 2026

Published July 2026 · Reading time: ~7 min

Fast Company recently ran a piece on the four tasks solopreneurs should already be handing to AI. The problem isn't whether AI can help — it's that most one-person businesses still open a blank ChatGPT window and type "help me with my business," then get generic mush back. The leverage is in the prompt, not the model. A precise prompt turns a 45-minute task into a 3-minute one; a vague one just wastes tokens.

Below are 15 battle-tested prompts, grouped by the jobs a solopreneur actually does every week. Copy them, swap the [bracketed] parts for your own details, and paste into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. They're model-agnostic. If you'd rather skip the setup, there's a free 10-prompt starter pack at the end.

1. Plan & Triage Your Week

The single highest-return prompt for a solo operator. Instead of staring at a to-do list of 30 items, you get a ranked plan tied to revenue.

Weekly triage prompt

You are my operations chief of staff. Here is my raw task dump for the week: [paste everything on your mind — tasks, half-ideas, follow-ups] Sort these into exactly 4 buckets: (1) Do now — directly makes or protects revenue this week, (2) Schedule — important but not urgent, (3) Delegate/automate — could be handled by a tool or template, (4) Delete — low value, be ruthless. For each "Do now" item, add a one-line first action so I can start without thinking. End with the single task that matters most this week.

Daily focus prompt

Based on this list of open work: [paste today's tasks], pick the ONE task that, if it were the only thing I did today, would make today a win. Then give me a 3-step plan to finish it before noon. Keep it under 120 words.

2. Price Your Work Without Second-Guessing

Underpricing is the quiet killer of one-person businesses. Use AI as a pricing sparring partner, not an oracle.

Quote-building prompt

Act as a pricing consultant for freelancers. I offer [service]. A client wants [scope of work], timeline [X weeks], and they're a [startup / SMB / enterprise]. My current rate is [rate]. Give me three price tiers (good / better / best), what each includes, and the one sentence I should say to anchor them to the middle tier. Flag any scope creep risks in the request.

"Am I charging enough?" prompt

Here's my offer and price: [describe offer + price]. Play devil's advocate: list 5 reasons this is priced too low, and 2 reasons a buyer might hesitate. Then suggest one value-add I could bundle to justify a 20-30% higher price.

3. Write Outreach & Replies in Your Voice

Cold outreach and inbox triage eat hours. These prompts keep the volume up without sounding like a robot.

Cold outreach prompt

Write a 90-word cold email to [name/role] at [company]. I offer [service] and I noticed [specific, real observation about their business]. Goal: get a 15-minute call. No fluff, no "I hope this finds you well," one clear ask, and a P.S. with social proof. Give me 2 subject-line options under 6 words.

Inbox reply prompt

Draft a reply to this message: [paste email]. Match this tone: friendly, direct, a little warm, no corporate jargon. If they're asking for a discount, hold the price but offer a smaller scope instead. Keep it under 100 words.

4. Turn One Idea Into a Week of Content

Content is how a solopreneur gets found — but making it from scratch daily is unsustainable. Repurpose instead.

Repurposing prompt

Here's a piece of content I already made: [paste blog post / transcript / long note]. Turn it into: (1) 3 short posts for X/LinkedIn with a hook first line, (2) one 5-bullet email, (3) a 4-line "carousel" outline, (4) 5 SEO title options. Keep my voice: [describe your voice in 3 words]. No hashtags, no emojis unless they add meaning.

Content idea prompt

I help [audience] achieve [outcome]. Give me 10 content angles that answer a real question my audience Googles at 11pm. For each, write the exact search-intent question and a one-line promise. Rank them by how likely they are to get saved/shared.

5. Debrief & Improve — the Prompt Most People Skip

The compounding advantage of running solo is a tight feedback loop. Close it every Friday.

Weekly debrief prompt

Here's what happened this week: [wins, misses, revenue, what drained me]. Act as a no-BS business coach. Tell me: (1) the one thing I should do MORE of, (2) the one thing to STOP, (3) one small experiment for next week. Then give me a single sentence to keep me focused Monday morning.

How to Actually Use These (So They Stick)

Three rules separate people who get 10x from AI from people who get frustrated and quit:

1. Give it your real data. The bracketed placeholders aren't optional. A prompt with your actual client, rate, and context beats a "perfect" generic prompt every time.

2. Save the ones that work. The whole point of a prompt library is that you never start from scratch. Keep a note (or a pack) of your best 15-20 prompts and reuse them weekly.

3. Chain them. Triage on Monday → outreach midweek → repurpose content → debrief Friday. The prompts compound when they're a system, not one-offs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best ChatGPT prompts for solopreneurs?

The highest-leverage prompts replace an entire recurring workflow: a weekly triage prompt, a pricing/quoting prompt, a cold-outreach prompt, and a content-repurposing prompt. Each turns a 30-60 minute task into a 3-minute one, which is where the real time savings come from.

Can AI really run a one-person business?

AI won't make decisions for you, but it will draft, triage, summarize, price, and repurpose. In practice a solopreneur with a good prompt library reclaims 5-10 hours a week that used to disappear into admin and busywork — time you redirect to selling and building.

Do these prompts work in both ChatGPT and Claude?

Yes — every prompt above is model-agnostic. Paste them into ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any modern LLM and just replace the bracketed placeholders with your own details.
